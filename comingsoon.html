DEGA

STORE

DEGA Store is a censorship-resistant and permissionless game store, where only you have the power to stop yourself from selling your game. You can buy whatever game you want, and so long as you have your receipt (proof-of-purchase), your bought games will always be with you. DEGA Store isn't here to be the middle-man, it's here only to ensure that ultimately both the buyer and seller call the shots between each other without interference.

Key points

Censorship-resistance

We can't delete your account, and we can't properly delete your store, your game product pages, and we can't properly stop you from selling your games. We also can't stop people from buying whatever they want.

Permissionless

You don't need our permission to make an account, sell, or post anything.

Moderation

While we can't delete your account, pages, or posts, or stop you from selling your games, and stop people from buying games, we can mark, hide, block pages and posts, though all moderation efforts can be bypassed by sellers and buyers. This results in both censorship-resistance and quality-control.

Interconnected stores

Game producers can setup their own store on their website that they control, and it would appear both on their site as well as on DEGA Store. So even if we block a game from being sold, it can still be bought on the producer's site.

Multiple payment methods

Having the ability to sell your games using whatever payment method that we're able to integrate into DEGA Store's system. Bitcoin will of course be implemented, and depending on the technicalities of traditional payment systems, they'd be implemented, as if one denies you service, you'd have another available, and so on. (implementation is one thing, your setup of each payment method is another thing).

No revenue split

Since we're not a middle-man between a buyer and seller, and payment setup is handled by the seller, the game producer gets 100% of a sale and DEGA Store doesn't get a cut. DEGA Store's revenue stream is acquired elsewhere.

No single-point of failure

Since DEGA Store isn't in control of the account creations or control them, and it only has copies of pages and posts and builds (assuming the studio set things up correctly), as well as not being the one selling games by not being the one in-between the buyer and seller, an attempt of a third-party to put pressure on DEGA Store to properly stop a game producer from selling their game is an imposibility, even if DEGA is under the threat of having its banking services revoked. Basically, think of DEGA Store as a browser. Also, in the scenario where DEGA Store goes under, sellers can still sell their game and the store would still be functional (a domain isn't needed, and backend reconfiguration and setup by the seller with their own resources can be done, so there can be 100% no reliance on DEGA Store).

Proof of purchase

or every pruchase, you have an ecrypted receipt, and so long as you have that receipt, it proof that you bought a specific game from a specific game producer, and it'll always be attached to your account and cannot be removed.

Ownership clarity

Clear wording of what the game producer is selling. Are they renting you their game with an unknown date of attempting to revoking access the game from you (licensing), or are they legitimately trying to sell you a game so you'd own a copy of it? Whichever is being sold or rented, you'll clearly know.

No DRM

DEGA Store won't have its own Digital Rights Management. Game producers can have their own DRM set in place, however, there's a checkbox that they'd tick if they do have it, so you'd know.

Game launcher

A launcher to manage your games and installs, with growing feature implementations.

Backup builds

Users have the ability to backup/mirror game builds of games they bought and have the ability to play that build (there's no guarantee that it'll work, as it depends on the developers of the game and how it was setup).

No forced updates

If there's an update for a game, you'll be informed to choose to update the game, and won't be prevented from playing the game if you choose not to update it (assuming that's possible with how a game produced developed the game).

FAQ

Isn't this too good to be true?

On the surface, probably, but not in reality. It's not all sunshine and rainbows, as this still takes decent effort to make things work, even for the game seller, in the background, but if all's done right and as this continues to grow, it'll get better, and freedoms continue to get more powerful.

Is all of this possible?

Yes. A lot of the systems that'll be developed for DEGA Store has already been developed elsewhere in different projects. The only system we're not 100% sure on is the multiple payment methods system, as we haven't dabbled with that yet, but we're optimistic about it and we'll update this answer with the good news in the future.

Since you aren't taking any cut from game sales, not even 1%, how are you able to make money to sustain DEGA Store?

Developers need storage and bandwidth for their game builds, as well as quality speeds, so we'll be selling that. Basically good backend infrastructure. That's the initial core of the business model, and there are more revenue streams to be implemented.

When will this go live?

We're currently focused on a different project, and DEGA Store is definitely a bigger endeavor than that other project, so as soon as that project starts getting profits with decent margins, a percentage of it will be used to fund the development of DEGA Store, or if investors come along to fund it. However, keep in mind that what's developed for that project will most likely be used for DEGA Store, so you could assume that we're already building it with that in mind. We're in the last stages of planning on this, and the frontend design is being developed in our free time whenever possible.
